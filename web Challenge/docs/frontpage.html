Characteristics of academic writing

Introduction

Akkaya and Aydin(2018:128) defines academic writing as “the process of sharing original research with other scholars in accordance with certain standard rules.“ It refers to a formal style of writing used in scholarly and university publications. ”An acceptable academic publication, should be ‘the first explanation’ that will provide sufficient information to the collegues to help evaluate observations, replicate experiments, and assesses intellectual operations” (Day, 2005:8).In simple terms, this implies that an academic writing is clear, concise, focused, structured and backed up by examples and evidence. Some of the characteristics of academic writing includes formality, explicitness, accuracy, and responsibility.

Formality

Firstly and foremost, an academic Writing uses formal language, that is, it does not include the use of colloquial words and expressions. Collaquil expressions are informal expressions that includes the use of slang terms such wicked. The degree of formality in academic writing should be high excluding the use of interjectory language that is used in daily conversations between friends and collegeaus.

Explicitness

Academic writing is explicit about the relationships in the text. This means that the academic writing should be clear , specific and detailed so that the intended audience can fully comprehense the depth the topic. Academic writing is clear about how various paths of the text are interconnected and why they are of importance to the central theme or topic. Signaling words are used and they act as signpost or roadmaps, showing the reader, the connection between ideas and thoughts. Examples of signaling words are in summary, for example, therefore, hence , however, conversely etc. It was noted by Clough(2002), that explicating ideas can also be through the use of narrative and fiction in qualitative academic writing.
